# Stonewall Playbook

This is the canonical GitHub markdown version of the Stonewall cheat sheet.

PDF export is optional and secondary.

## Core idea

The Stonewall is a structure-first opening family.

- **White:** `d4`, `e3`, `f4`, usually `Bd3`
- **Black:** `d5`, `e6`, `f5`, `c6`, usually `Bd6`

The recurring questions are:

- when is the central outpost ready?
- when should the bad bishop stay bad?
- when does the position justify direct kingside violence?

## White Stonewall

### Structure and priorities

- build `d4` and `e3`
- get `Bd3` developed
- choose between `f4` and `c3` based on the opponent's setup
- usually castle kingside

### Main plans

- **`Ne5`** — the default attacking square
- **`g4`** — the kingside pawn storm when the structure supports it
- **`e4`** — the central break that transforms the position
- **`Bxh7+` / `Bxh6`** — distinct bishop attack families
- **`Bd2 -> Be1 -> Bh4`** — repair route for the bad bishop when the normal attack is denied

### White practical rule

Do not autopilot `Nd2` in every structure. In symmetrical or KID-like setups, the bishop maneuver can matter more than the usual knight route.

## Black Stonewall

### Structure and priorities

- build the wall first in the normal `d4` Stonewall
- aim for `Ne4`
- solve the `c8` bishop problem only when the structure says you need to

### Main plans

- **`Ne4`** — central backbone of the position
- **`Bd7 -> Be8 -> Bh5`** — light-squared bishop maneuver
- **`...Qe8` reroutes** — support kingside pressure
- **`...g5`** — black's kingside storm
- **`Bxh2+`** — the mirror-image bishop sacrifice family

## Same system, different philosophy

What transfers cleanly between White and Black:

- central outpost play
- bad-bishop repair maneuvers
- kingside pawn storms
- sacrifice motifs on the `h`-pawn

What does not transfer cleanly:

- White often urgently cares about `...Nd4`; Black often tolerates `Ne5`
- White builds the attacking bishop early; Black often builds the wall first
- White's `g4` plan is more central than Black's `...g5`

## The key structural lessons

### Delay castling when the outpost solves a real problem

- White can play `Ne5` before castling to punish slow play, block a bishop, stop `...f5`, or sidestep a pin
- Black can play `Ne4` before castling to block `Re1+` or seize a weak `e4` square

### Use the bad-bishop maneuver only when the structure demands it

- White bishop maneuver is especially relevant versus symmetrical `...f5` or KID-style setups
- Black bishop maneuver is especially relevant versus symmetrical `f4` or KIA / g3 setups

### Respect the opponent's recurring anti-Stonewall ideas

Important warnings include:

- `...Bb7`
- `...b6`
- `...Nc6`
- `...Qb6`
- `...Bd6 + ...c5`
- queenside pawn expansion aiming for `...Ba6`

## Fast summary

### White

- build `Bd3`
- look for `Ne5`
- know when `g4` is correct
- know when `e4` is ripe
- repair the bad bishop only when the position blocks the normal attack

### Black

- build the wall first in the standard setup
- aim for `Ne4`
- treat the `c8` bishop as the main strategic problem
- use the bishop maneuver when the outpost plan is structurally denied
